VAO Acoustic Feature Descriptions
===================================
All features are smoothed (sma3 = 3-frame moving average).
Features marked (nz) are only defined for voiced frames.

--- ENERGY & LOUDNESS ---

Loudness_sma3
    Perceptual loudness based on the auditory filter bank. Near zero in silence,
    higher during speech.

--- SPECTRAL BALANCE ---

alphaRatio_sma3
    Ratio of energy in high (1-5 kHz) vs low (50-1000 Hz) frequency bands (dB).
    Low for sonorants (energy concentrated low), higher for noise/obstruents.

hammarbergIndex_sma3
    Ratio of energy in the 0-2 kHz band to 2-5 kHz band. Reflects dominance
    of the voice source harmonics. High for voiced speech, low for unvoiced.

slope0-500_sma3
    Linear regression slope of the spectrum between 0-500 Hz.
    Captures the tilt of the low-frequency spectral region.

slope500-1500_sma3
    Linear regression slope of the spectrum between 500-1500 Hz.
    Captures the mid-frequency spectral tilt.

spectralFlux_sma3
    How fast the spectrum is changing frame-to-frame. High during transitions
    and noisy consonants, lower during steady-state vowels.

--- CEPSTRAL (MFCC) ---

mfcc1_sma3
    First MFCC coefficient. Strongly correlated with overall spectral energy
    and the general shape of the vocal tract transfer function.

mfcc2_sma3
    Second MFCC. Captures the main spectral tilt (high vs low frequency balance).

mfcc3_sma3
    Third MFCC. Encodes mid-frequency spectral shape information.

mfcc4_sma3
    Fourth MFCC. Encodes finer spectral shape details.

--- F0 & VOICING (voiced frames only) ---

F0semitoneFrom27.5Hz_sma3nz  [sonorant only]
    Fundamental frequency (pitch) in semitones relative to 27.5 Hz (A0).
    NaN'd for obstruents and silence.

voicingFinalUnclipped
    Viterbi voicing probability (0-1) from the pitch tracker. Represents the
    model's confidence that the frame is voiced. Not smoothed.

--- VOICE QUALITY (voiced frames only) ---

jitterLocal_sma3nz  [sonorant only]
    Cycle-to-cycle variation in pitch period length, as a percentage.
    Measures pitch irregularity. Higher in pathological or creaky voice.
    NaN'd for obstruents and silence.

shimmerLocaldB_sma3nz  [sonorant only]
    Cycle-to-cycle variation in amplitude (dB). Measures amplitude irregularity.
    Higher in breathy or rough voice quality. NaN'd for obstruents and silence.

HNRdBACF_sma3nz
    Harmonics-to-Noise Ratio in dB. Measures how much of the signal is periodic
    vs noise. High for clean sonorants, low for obstruents and noise.

logRelF0-H1-H2_sma3nz  [sonorant only]
    Log ratio of the amplitude of the first harmonic (H1) to the second (H2).
    Correlates with breathiness — higher values indicate breathier voice.
    NaN'd for obstruents and silence.

logRelF0-H1-A3_sma3nz  [sonorant only]
    Log ratio of H1 amplitude to the amplitude of the third formant peak (A3).
    Another voice quality measure related to glottal open quotient.
    NaN'd for obstruents and silence.

--- FORMANTS (sonorant only) ---

F1frequency_sma3nz
    First formant frequency (Hz). Correlates with vowel height — low vowels
    (e.g. /a/) have high F1, high vowels (e.g. /i/) have low F1.

F1bandwidth_sma3nz
    Bandwidth of F1. Narrower bandwidths indicate clearer resonances.

F1amplitudeLogRelF0_sma3nz
    Amplitude of F1 relative to F0 (log scale). Reflects the strength of the
    first formant resonance.

F2frequency_sma3nz
    Second formant frequency (Hz). Correlates with vowel backness — front vowels
    (e.g. /i/) have high F2, back vowels (e.g. /u/) have low F2.

F2bandwidth_sma3nz
    Bandwidth of F2.

F2amplitudeLogRelF0_sma3nz
    Amplitude of F2 relative to F0 (log scale).

F3frequency_sma3nz
    Third formant frequency (Hz). Relates to lip rounding and individual
    speaker characteristics. Also important for /r/ sounds.

F3bandwidth_sma3nz
    Bandwidth of F3.

F3amplitudeLogRelF0_sma3nz
    Amplitude of F3 relative to F0 (log scale).

F4frequency_sma3nz
    Fourth formant frequency (Hz). Less commonly used but captures higher
    vocal tract resonances. Speaker-characteristic.

F4bandwidth_sma3nz
    Bandwidth of F4.

F5frequency_sma3nz
    Fifth formant frequency (Hz). Rarely used in phonetic analysis but
    included for completeness.

F5bandwidth_sma3nz
    Bandwidth of F5.

--- SPECTRAL SHAPE (ComParE-derived) ---

pcm_fftMag_spectralRollOff25.0_sma
    Frequency below which 25% of the total spectral energy is contained.

pcm_fftMag_spectralRollOff50.0_sma
    Frequency below which 50% of the total spectral energy is contained
    (spectral median).

pcm_fftMag_spectralRollOff75.0_sma
    Frequency below which 75% of the total spectral energy is contained.

pcm_fftMag_spectralRollOff90.0_sma
    Frequency below which 90% of the total spectral energy is contained.

pcm_fftMag_spectralCentroid_sma
    Center of mass of the spectrum (weighted mean frequency). High for
    fricatives and high-frequency sounds, lower for voiced speech.

pcm_fftMag_spectralEntropy_sma
    Entropy of the normalized spectrum. Low for periodic sounds with clear
    harmonics (sonorants), high for noise-like sounds (obstruents).

pcm_fftMag_spectralVariance_sma
    Variance of the spectral distribution around the centroid. Measures
    how spread out the energy is across frequencies.

pcm_fftMag_spectralSkewness_sma
    Skewness of the spectral distribution. Positive = energy concentrated
    at lower frequencies, negative = concentrated at higher frequencies.

pcm_fftMag_spectralKurtosis_sma
    Kurtosis of the spectral distribution. High kurtosis indicates a
    peaked spectrum (narrow resonances); low indicates a flat spectrum.

pcm_fftMag_spectralSlope_sma
    Overall linear regression slope of the FFT magnitude spectrum. Steep
    negative slope for voiced speech, flatter for noise and fricatives.

pcm_fftMag_psySharpness_sma
    Psychoacoustic sharpness — a perceptual measure of how "sharp" or
    high-frequency dominated the sound is. High for fricatives, low for vowels.

pcm_fftMag_spectralHarmonicity_sma
    Degree to which the spectrum follows a harmonic structure. High for
    periodic voiced sounds, low for aperiodic noise.
